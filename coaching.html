1:1 Coaching

Individual sessions for focused relational work

The same relational approach as cohort work, but in a dyadic container. One-on-one sessions are for people who need individual attention — whether as preparation for group work, concurrent support during a cohort, or standalone practice when group timing doesn't fit.

What's different from the cohort

The mechanism is the same: patterns formed in relationships shift in relationships. But the container is different.

In a cohort, the relational field is distributed across multiple people. You see your pattern reflected in others. You practice in front of witnesses. The work happens through peer interaction.

In 1:1 sessions, the relational field is concentrated in the dyad — just you and me. This creates space for:

  • More individualized attention to your specific pattern
  • Deeper exploration of resistance or stuck points
  • Working with material that feels too vulnerable for group process
  • Faster cycles of noticing and adjusting

What doesn't change: this is still peer-based work, not therapy. I'm not diagnosing or fixing. We're practicing together in real time.

What happens in a session

Sessions are 70 minutes. We work with what's present — what you're noticing in your life, what's showing up in the room between us, what patterns are running right now.

The work is embodied and relational. We're not just talking about your patterns. We're working with them as they arise — in your body, in the interaction, in real time.

Between sessions, you practice. The work doesn't stop when the session ends — it continues in your relationships, your daily life, the moments when the pattern tries to run automatically.

Who this is for

1:1 sessions work well if:

  • You're preparing for cohort work and want to build foundational awareness first
  • You're in a cohort and need additional support with specific stuck points
  • You've completed cohort work and want ongoing practice
  • Group timing doesn't work but you're committed to the practice
  • You need space to work with material that feels too vulnerable for group process

This isn't a substitute for cohort work if you're avoiding the group container. If you're capable of group work, that's where the deepest shifts happen. But if you genuinely need individual attention, 1:1 sessions can be valuable.
